The 17 Best High-Waisted Leggings of 2024

Publish date: 2024-08-17

High-rise leggings are like the miracle workers of the pant world. They smooth your stomach, lift your booty, and create the illusion of longer legs. And who wants to wear a sports bra with low-rise leggings? Not us.

Yet finding the perfect high-waisted pair isn’t easy. With so many brands out there today, it’s hard to decipher which styles are worth the money over which ones roll, pinch, or simply show too much skin. So we spoke with fitness experts and then researched the best leggings in high quality materials, extensive size ranges and inseams, and various compression levels. Whether you’re looking to lounge in a pair of high-waisted leggings, want one for your most intense workout sessions, or need a few for your growing belly as a mom-to-be, we found the best pairs that you can add to your virtual cart right now.

Best Overall

What We Don’t Love: The 28-inch inseam may be too long for petite figures.

There’s nothing quite like a buttery pair of leggings, and Alo Yoga’s High-Waist Airlift Leggings is the best on the market in our book. Constructed with an unbeatable softness and four-way stretch, these leggings will move with you during your workouts and errand runs. The extra-wide waistband sculpts and smooths while the high compression material lifts your booty for extra confidence with every step.

Further, you can trust the durability and excellence of this style thanks to Alo’s initiative to wear-test their products in-house—a definite bonus once you consider its higher price point. We also love the sheer number of colorways to choose from; there are three neutral “core” options and 12 brighter, bolder hues (like teal green and fluorescent pink) for you to peruse before you add to cart. With a 28 inch inseam, the legging may be a bit long for shorter folks, so you may want to consider the ⅞ High-Waist Airlift Legging instead.

What to Keep in Mind

Material

The material makeup of a legging has a big impact on its functionality. For example, leggings made primarily from cotton, like the Skims Cotton Rib Leggings, are ideal for lounging, but likely too soft and sheer for a workout. If you’re looking for leggings that will withstand any sort of activity, consider materials like nylon, spandex, or elastane, which can be found in pairs like P.E Nation’s Recalibrate Legging or Hey Girl Run’s High-Rise 25” Legging. 

Stylist Alison Bruhn also recommends looking for sweat-wicking fabrics when selecting a pair for exercise.

Length

The length of your leggings, or inseam, is one of the more important factors to keep in mind, especially if you’re on the extra-short or extra-tall side. Thankfully, a lot of brands offer styles in regular, petite, and tall inseams to cater to all ends of the height spectrum. But a good rule of thumb for petites is to look for leggings with inseams that fall between 19 to 24 inches, like Athleta’s Ultra High-Rise Elation 7/8 Tight. Those who are tall should look for inseams anywhere between 28 to 31 inches. When in doubt, check the size guide and reviews to see if other customers discuss the length of the leggings.

Waist Height

While all of the picks on this list feature a high-rise waistband, not all brands have the same idea of “high-rise” in mind. A traditional high-waisted pair will hit either at your belly button or just above, as seen in styles like Vuori’s Daily Pocket Legging. An ultra-high-rise pair, on the other hand, will just about reach your chest area, like we see in Old Navy’s Extra High-Waisted PowerChill Hidden-Pocket Leggings or Hatch’s The Ultra Soft Before, During & After Legging. 

Your Questions, Answered

Does a high waist keep the leggings from rolling down?

Yes and no. While higher waistbands made from supportive fabric should stay put, according to Liotta, rolling often is an indication that you are wearing the wrong size. So before cutting off the price tag, it’s best to try the leggings out. 

"Bend over, stretch your hands over your head, run in place,” says Bruhn. “If at any point, the waist starts rolling down, they are not the best fit for you. If it is a little compression and support that you’re looking for, find a wider band that goes across the stomach.”

Are high-waisted leggings compressive?

While high-waist bands tend to offer more smoothing and support, that doesn’t necessarily mean the leggings are meant to be compressive, according to Bruhn. To ensure you get all the sculpting you want, look for leggings with high compression levels, like Girlfriend Collective’s Compressive High-Rise Legging. 

What underwear should you wear under high-waisted leggings?

While underwear preference is totally up to whatever the wearer feels most comfortable in, Bruhn recommends wearing a thong in order to have the most seamless look with no panty lines. If you’re worried about missing the coverage that panties provide, have no fear. “The stretchy fabric from the legging should naturally hold you in, creating a smooth look with plenty of coverage,” says Bruhn. 

Even thongs can create panty lines — we’re looking at you, lacy designs — so it’s best to stick with lightweight, seamless options. Liotta recommends Lululemon’s InvisiWear Mid-Rise Thong Underwear, which is made from a sweat-wicking, breathable fabric and has a cotton gusset that will keep you comfortable no matter the workout.
