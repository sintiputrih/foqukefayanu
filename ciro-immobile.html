Ciro Immobile Biography

Ciro Immobile was born on February 20, 1990, in Torre Annunziata, Italy. Antonio Immobile is his father (dad), and Michela Immobile is his mother (mum). No information is available about his father’s or mother’s professional backgrounds. Luigi Immobile is his brother.

Ciro was the most gifted child among his peers when he was a child. He had always wanted to play football. His parents, Antonio and Michela Immobile, were also very supportive of their son. When this football genius was five years old, his parents enrolled him in the Torre Annunziata”88″ football school. When he was 12 years old, he began his youth career with the Italian association football club Sorrento.

Career

  • After the 2008-09 season, Immobile signed with Juventus at the age of 18. From 2009 to 2012, he appeared in three games for the Bianconeri. In 2010, the Old Lady loaned him to the Siena. He made four appearances for the Robur and scored one goal before being transferred to Grosseto in 2011. He appeared in 16 games for the Tuscany team, scoring once.
  • He moved to Pescara before the start of the 2011-12 season, and by the end of the season, he was the top scorer in Serie B, with 28 goals in 37 appearances.
  • He then spent the 2012-13 season with Genoa, appearing in 33 games and scoring five goals. In 2013, Immobile joined Juventus’ city rival Torino, where he would make 33 appearances and score 22 goals
  • On June 2, 2014, the German club Borussia Dortmund confirmed Immobile’s signing. Two months later, he made his official Dortmund debut, starting against Bayern Munich in the 2014 DFL Supercup.
  • He played an important role in the game that saw the BVB win the cup by a score of 2-0. In 24 appearances for the Germans, he scored three goals. He began playing for the Spanish club Sevilla after the 2014-15 season ended. He appeared in eight games and scored two goals for Los Palanganas. He returned to Torino in the middle of the 2015-16 season and scored five goals in 14 appearances.
  • On July 27, 2016, the Annunziata-born striker joined Lazio. Immobile had 123 goals in 177 games across all competitions as of June 2021. Along the way, he won the Coppa Italia (2018-19) and SuperCoppa Italiana (2017, 2019). Immobile has made 46 appearances for the national team, scoring 13 goals.

Interesting Facts

  • Juventus scouts recommended him to the club management after he scored 30 goals in the under-17 category during the 2007-2008 season. In 2008, the club paid €80,000 to sign 18-year-old Immobile.
  • Immobile made his debut in the 2011-12 season after Juventus loaned him to Serie B side Pescara. He scored 28 league goals for the club and helped them win Serie B, which led to their promotion to Serie A.
  • Midway through his season with Pescara, he impressed Genoa management enough that they agreed to pay Juventus €4 million for half of the player’s rights in January 2012.
  • He was named Serie B’s Player of the Year at the end of the 2011-12 season, alongside Pescara teammates Marco Verratti and Lorenzo Insigne. He was also the league’s leading scorer.
  • In July 2013, Juventus agreed to pay Genoa €2.75 million for Immobile’s rights. They immediately accepted Torino’s offer of €2.75 million for one-half of the player’s rights.
