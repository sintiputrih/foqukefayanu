David Blaine - Bio, Career, Single, Age, Net Worth, Height, Facts

Table of Biography

David Blaine is a magician, illusionist, extreme performer, and endurance artist from New York City, the United States. Moreover, David Blaine is popular for his endurance stunts and as a performer of street and close-up magic.

Early Life

David Blaine was born in Brooklyn, New York City, United States on April 4, 1973. He was born under the zodiac sign Aries and his current age is 50 years old as of 2022. Moreover, he holds American nationality by birth and belongs to the mixed ethnicity of  Italian, American, Russian-Jewish, and Puerto Rican ancestry. His father Willaim Perez is a veteran of the Vietnam War and his mother Patrice Maureen White is a school teacher.

Moreover, he is the only son of his parents and his mother raised him while working at several jobs to fulfill their daily needs. At the age of four, he saw a magician performing tricks in the subway and developed an interest in it. Moreover, he started practicing magic himself with his grandmother’s gifted tarot cards.

David Blaine studied and completed his education at the Passaic Valley Regional High School. After that, he developed an interest in acting and went to an acting school in Manhattan, New York City.

Professional life

Caption: David performing stunt called Frozen in Time (Source: The Times)

David decided to devote his life to magic by the time he was 20 years old. However, his mother was supportive of his decision but his mother passed away due to cancer when he was 21 years old. After that, he started as a street performer and gained popularity due to his tricks and charismatic personality. Later, he sent his performance video to NBC after his success as a street magician.

On May 19, 1997, the channel aired Blaine’s first television special “‘David Blaine Street Magic” after his interview for the channel. After two years, the channel aired another television special “David Magic Man” which became very successful. Moreover, he traveled across the country and performed tricks in the streets before real people for his shows.

Furthermore, he became a national celebrity after his performances in Atlantic City, Compton, Dallas, the Mojave Desert, New York City, and San Francisco. In 1999, he performed his first endurance stunt when he submerged himself in a 3-ton water-filled tank for seven days. Due to this trick, he gained a huge fan following as he had outdone his hero Harry Houdini. After that, he performed a stunt named “Frozen in Time” in 2000 in which he was frozen into a block of ice for around 63 hours. Likewise, is another most popular magic as he was locked inside a 3x7x7-foot transparent Plexiglass box in the tower which was suspended 30ft in the air. He was there for six weeks without any food.

He began his 44-day endurance stunt sealed inside a transparent Plexiglas case suspended 9 meters three years later on September 5, 2003. Moreover, he emerged from the case murmuring “I love you all” on October 19, 2003. He performed stunts like “Dive of Death” and “Electrified One Million Volts Always On” over the next few years.

Performing For Celebrities and Public Figures and Philanthropic Works

Later, he appeared in the 90-minute ABC television special “David Real or Magic” in 2013. Moreover, he performed his tricks for celebrities and public figures like Woody Allen, Robert De Niro, George W Bush, Bryan Cranston, Aaron Paul, Harrison Ford, Kanye West, Jamie Foxx, and Ricky Gervais. Additionally, he also performs magic at children’s wards, burns units, and juvenile wards in hospitals all over the country.

Moreover, he performed for children diagnosed with serious illnesses at the Hole in the Wall Gang Camp. In January 2010, he performed for 72 hours in the “Magic for Haiti” and raised nearly $100,000. Moreover, he also performed for the ABC channel on his shows like “Real or Magic”, “Beyond Magic”, and “The Magic Way”.

Achievements

In 2002, David Blaine received the Magician of the Year Award from the Academy of Magical Arts. He again received the Magician of the Year Award in 2018 but declined the award due to unknown reasons.
