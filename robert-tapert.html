How did Robert start his Professional Career?

During his time at Michigan State University, Robert Tapert was introduced to filmmaking by his roommate Sam Raimi. They worked together on “The Happy Valley Kid,” a story about a young man, played by Tapert, who attends college only to be rejected at every turn. Following the success of his first film, he produced “The Evil Dead,” a violent horror film directed by his friend Sam Raimi.

As a result of the horror film, he rose to international prominence. Later in his career, he began producing films like “Darkman,” “Crimewave,” “Timecop,” “The Gift,” and others. He has also created television shows like “Hercules: The Legendary Journey”, “Xena: Warrior Princess”, and “Young Hercules”. In 2015, Tapert returned to the Evil Dead franchise with the Starz series Ash versus Evil Dead. He is currently working on two new drama series, “Airborne” and “Xena”.
