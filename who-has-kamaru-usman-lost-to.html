Who has Kamaru Usman lost to?

Publish date: 2024-09-07

Kamaru Usman, the dominant UFC champion, has faced only one loss in his professional career. He lost to Leon Edwards in the main event of UFC 278. Despite the loss, Usman remains unfazed, stating in an interview that he is not bothered by the defeat. He acknowledged Edwards' knockout shot in the final minute of the fifth round and praised his opponent for landing a great shot. Usman's confidence remains intact, and he believes he should get an immediate rematch against Edwards. The article suggests that Usman should be favored in the rematch based on his performance prior to the knockout.

In addition to his loss to Edwards, it is worth mentioning that Usman has faced various challenges on his journey to becoming the UFC champion. After returning from UFC 251 in July 2020, Usman contracted COVID-19 and lost 17 pounds during his recovery. This setback did not deter him, as he has proven to be a resilient and determined fighter throughout his career.

Usman's personality is often described as enigmatic and inward. He tends to keep important aspects of his life to himself, only revealing surface truths in his interviews. His single-mindedness and focus are the sources of his complexity. Usman draws inspiration from his father, who left Nigeria in 1989 to pursue an advanced degree in the US. Usman vividly remembers meeting his father for the first time when he was around 4 years old, and his father's achievements continue to awe him.

Despite the loss to Edwards, Usman's record speaks for itself. He is currently the UFC's most dominant champion, and his skill and determination have earned him a rightful place among the top fighters in the world. Usman's journey is a testament to his resilience, and he remains a strong contender in the UFC welterweight division.

Looking ahead, Usman is set to face Jorge Masvidal in a rematch at UFC 261. This fight presents another opportunity for Usman to showcase his skills and cement his status as one of the greatest fighters in the UFC. As the dominant champion, Usman's dedication and unwavering focus will undoubtedly play a crucial role in his future fights.
