Chevy Chase

In Chevy’s early life, he co-founded ‘Channel One,’ a comedy group in 1967. He was a drummer and keyboardist for the aspiring rock band, ‘Chameleon Church.’ Before breaking down in 1969, the band released only one album. Following his musical career, he created a parody of ‘Mission: Impossible’ for ‘MAD’ magazine.

In 1973, he joined National Lampoon’s off-Broadway production of ‘Lemmings.’ From 1973 to 1974, he appeared on ‘The National Lampoon Radio Hour,’ alongside John Belushi, Bill Murray, and Gilda Radner. In 1975, he was hired as a writer for NBC’s ‘Saturday Night Live.’ He went on to become a member of the show’s main cast.

He was on his way to Hollywood a year later. In 1978, he was nominated for a ‘Golden Globe’ for his film debut, ‘Foul Play.’ He appeared in the film ‘Modern Problems’ in 1980. He almost avoided electrocution while shooting.

He also appeared in the sports comedy film ‘Caddyshack’ the same year. ‘National Lampoon’s Vacation’ (1983), the first film in the ‘National Lampoon’ series, proved to be a defining moment in his career. In 1985, he began working in ‘Fletch.’ It was the first film adaptation of Gregory Mcdonald’s novel series ‘Fletch.’

Raise of Fame

Around 1987 and 1988, he reached that level of his profession and began charging excessive fees for his work. He and his friend Simon were featured in the song “You Can Call Me Al.” In 1987 and 1988, he also presented the ‘Academy Awards.’

In 1989, He shot the second and third episodes of the ‘National Lampoon’ series, titled ‘National Lampoon’s European Vacation’ and ‘National Lampoon’s Christmas Vacation,’ respectively. His career suffered throughout the 1990s when films like ‘Nothing But Trouble,’ ‘Memoirs of an Invisible Man,’ and ‘Cops & Robbersons’ flopped at the box office.

Comeback to films and television

In 1993, he hosted ‘The Chevy Chase Show.’ The show, a ‘Fox Broadcasting Company,’ production, was pulled off the air after five weeks. In 1995, he made a return with the film ‘Man of the House,’ which performed better at the box office than his prior efforts. After nearly a decade apart, he returned to television with guest parts on series such as ‘Law & Order’ and ‘Brothers & Sisters.’

He also played ‘Ted Roark’ in the comedy film ‘Chuck.’ In 2009, he appeared as a tycoon named ‘Pierce Hawthorne’ in the comedy series ‘Community.’ In 2010, he appeared in the films, ‘Hot Tub Time Machine’ and ‘Funny or Die.’
