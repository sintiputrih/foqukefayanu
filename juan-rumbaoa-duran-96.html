Juan Rumbaoa Duran, 96 | News, Sports, Jobs

Publish date: 2024-08-05

Juan Rumbaoa Duran, 96, of Pukalani, Maui, peacefully passed away on Tuesday, December 22, 2020. He was born in 25 Bacarra Ilocos Norte, Philippines, on July 12, 1924, to Gre-gorio and Diega Duran.

He is survived by his loving wife, Agrifina Duran; son, Victoriano (Antonia) Duran; and daughter, Marina (Antonio) Sagario; siblings Alfredo (Erlinda) Duran, Mary (Victorio -deceased) Castillo; Marcela (Isaias) Lagundino, and Andrea (Paul) Cacal, Jr.; grandchildren, Victor Duran, Bradley (Jamie) Duran, Dawn (Nathan) Pauls, and Jenny (Zachary) Tokushima, along with four great- grandchil-dren.

He is predeceased by his parents and brother Epifanio (Margarita) Duran.

The family would like to express gratitude for the support and care received from Bayada Home Care, Islands Hospice and Dr. Noel Termulo.

Services will be held at Norman’s Mortuary with burial to follow at Valley Isle Memorial Park Cemetery. Visitation will take place Saturday, Jan. 16, from 9 a.m. to 10:30 a.m. at Norman’s Mortuary, 105 Waiale Rd., Wailuku, Maui, Hawaii, 96793. Each visitor’s temperature will be taken at the door. A private service will fol-low due to COVID-19 restrictions. The burial will take place at 1 p.m. at Valley Isle Memorial Park Cemetery, Hana Hwy, Haiku, Maui, Hawaii. Visitors must remain in their cars during the burial.

Norman’s Mortuary Online Condolences to: www.ballardfamilymortuaries.com
