Juan Angel Montes and Maria Rosaria Montes Dorantes: Tragic Siblings Found Dead on I-85 in Gwinnett

In a heartbreaking incident, a brother and sister, Juan Angel Montes and Maria Rosaria Montes Dorantes, were discovered lifeless on the shoulder of Interstate 85, near the I-985 junction. Gwinnett County Police are investigating this disturbing occurrence that unfolded on a Sunday evening.

Responding to an unknown medical call around 7:30 p.m., officers were met with the grim discovery of the two deceased individuals in the grass adjacent to the interstate. Strikingly, no vehicles were present near them, intensifying the mystery of the situation.

The location of the tragic event lies between Buford and Suwanee, with closer proximity to Suwanee, according to Gwinnett County Police’s communication with Patch.

Through meticulous investigation, the Gwinnett County Medical Examiner’s Office concluded that the cause of their deaths was gunshot wounds, resulting in a heartbreaking double homicide. The victims have been identified as Maria Rosaria Montes Dorantes, 23, and her brother, Juan Angel Montes, 16, hailing from Griffin. The exact residence of Dorantes remains undisclosed.

As the authorities earnestly pursue leads and solicit tips, the community is left in shock, grappling with unanswered questions about the circumstances leading to this tragic shooting. The Gwinnett County Police urge anyone with information pertinent to the case to promptly contact detectives at (770) 513-5300. Those preferring anonymity can contribute by reaching out to Crime Stoppers Atlanta at (404) 577-8477.
