ControversyAccused Salim Merchant of Copying his Song
In 2019, Farhan made a tweet in which accused the Indian film director Salim Merchant of copying the notes of his song “Roiyaan” in the Hindi song “Haareya.” [4] Farhan tweeted,
“Someone just sent me @salim_merchant song HAREYA, which is a total copy of my song ROIYAAN. I wonder they have the audacity to call themselves artists when they steal someone’s work. Karna hi hai to pooch ke kuro aur ager poochna nahin hai to at least acha to kuro! #stopstealing.”
His tweet went viral and grabbed the attention of Salim Merchant. Salim then retweeted,
“Farhan I just heard your song. It’s a mere coincidence that the chorus of Haareya is like your song. To be honest I’ve never heard it before. It happens many a times when notes have a natural progression from each other. @Sulaiman & I have a track record of never plagiarising.”
Farhan was not convinced with Salim’s answer and retweeted,
“@salim_merchant if you say so! Another co-incidence is we have the same lyricist! Anyway good luck!”
Later, Salim tweeted,
“Why don’t you check with him! If i had to copy, I would have done that way long back in my career. I really wish I had heard your song before I made mine. Would have surely made changes to our composition to make it sound different from yours. Anyway, Hope you understand.”

Some Lesser Known Facts About Farhan Saeed

  • Farhan Saeed is a Pakistani singer, songwriter, music video director, actor, and entrepreneur.
  • While he was studying in school, he used to listen to the music of the Pakistani music bands Vital Signs and Junoon. Since then, he started developing an interest in music.

    Farhan Saeed’s childhood picture

  • In December 2003, after the Pakistani singer Atif Aslam left the Pakistani music band ‘Jal,’ the co-founder of the band Goher Mumtaz added two new members to the band including Farhan as a vocalist and Aamir Tufail as a bass guitarist.

    Farhan Saeed with the members of Jal music band

  • In 2004, the band released its first music album ‘Aadat’ including songs such as “Dil Haray Pukaray,” “Ik Din Ayega,” “Teri Yaad,” and “Bikhra Hoon Mein.”
  • The band released its second music album ‘Lamhay’ in October 2004. ‘Jal’ released another Pakistani music album ‘Boondh’ (2007) with the songs like “Sajni,” “Raatein,” “Moré Piya,” “Main Mast Hoon,” and “Mahia.”

    Boondh music album

  • Farhan announced his separation from the band on 8 September 2011, to make his career as a solo singer. During an interview, while talking about ‘Jal,’ he said,Jal was one of the best time periods of my life. It seems like some other life now. I spent ten years with the band. Some of the best times of my life were with Jal. The band life is, in itself, a great thing to experience. And yes, we had a lot of fun and Alhamdulillah, by the grace of God, we saw the best times as a band. We toured the world, we saw hardships together and we experienced [success] together. I was a teen when I joined Jal, and I left when I was 28, so a decade of my life, my prime youth, was with Jal, and I loved every bit of it! I miss it sometimes, but it’s beautiful and I want it to be like that in my memories forever.”
  • He has lent his voice to many Hindi film songs like “Naam-e-Wafa” from ‘Creature 3D’ (2014), “Bhool Na Jaana” from ‘Main Aur Mr. Riight,’ and “Thodi Der” from ‘Half Girlfriend’ (2017).
  • Some of his popular Pakistani songs are “Khuwahishon” (2011), “Roiyaan” (2014), “Haari Haari” (2018), and “Piyaar Sufiyana” (2021).
  • He has been invited to perform live at various stage shows and events.

    Farhan Saeed performing on stage

  • Farhan has worked as an actor in various Pakistani TV dramas such as ‘Udaari’ (2016), ‘Suno Chanda’ (2018), ‘Prem Gali’ (2020), ‘Mere Humsafar’ (2021), and ‘Badshah Begum’ (2022).

    Farhan Saeed as Hamza in Mere Humsafar (2021)
