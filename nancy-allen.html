Nancy Allen Biography

Nancy Allen is an American actress who was born and brought up in The Bronx, New York City, U.S. She became popular for her performances in several films directed by Brian De Palma in the 1970s and early 1980s. Allen garnered mainstream fame playing Anne Lewis in Paul Verhoeven’s RoboCop (1987), a role she reprised for the two sequels that followed. Her other credits include Poltergeist III (1988), Limit Up (1990), Les patriotes (1994) amongst many others. She has also won several accolades including a Golden Globe Award nomination and three Saturn Award nominations.

    Nancy Allen Actress

    Straight from school, she entered into the acting industry appearing in 1962 until 2012. Her first major film role was as Nancy, Jack Nicholson’s nervous date, in The Last Detail (1973). This inspired her to move to Los Angeles to continue her acting career.

    Subsequently, she appeared in the role of Pam Mitchell in Steven Spielberg’s production of I Wanna Hold Your Hand (1978), She then played Donna Stratton in the Spielberg-directed comedy 1941 (1979) opposite Tim Matheson, John Belushi, Dan Aykroyd, and John Candy.

    Her television guest appearances include roles on Touched by an Angel, The Outer Limits, The Commish, and Law & Order: Special Victims Unit.

    Nancy Allen Robocop

    Allen was cast as police officer Anne Lewis in the science fiction/action classic RoboCop (1987) opposite Peter Weller in the title role. The film did extremely well at the box office. Allen was nominated for a Saturn Award for Best Actress.

    The film’s plot is set in a crime-ridden Detroit, Michigan, in the near future. It revolves around police officer Alex Murphy (Weller) who is murdered by a gang of criminals and subsequently revived by the megacorporation Omni Consumer Products as the cyborg law enforcer RoboCop.

    Unaware of his former life, RoboCop executes a brutal campaign against crime while coming to terms with the lingering fragments of his humanity.
