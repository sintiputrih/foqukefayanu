All About Broadway Star Chris Peluso

Chris Peluso (age: 38, July 1, 1985 – August 16, 2023) was a dynamic and gifted Broadway actor whose influence resonated deeply throughout the performing arts world.

Renowned for his compelling stage presence and extraordinary talents, Peluso’s artistic journey left an indelible mark in US and Uk.

Chris started his acting career in a Broadway show called “Assassins.” He also played a lead role in the popular show “Wicked,” which went on tour across the country.

However, Peluso made his mark with his performance as Sky in “Mamma Mia,” Fiyero in “Wicked,” Marius in “Les Misérables,” and Tony in “West Side Story.”

Despite his success, Chris faced challenges in his personal life. He had a condition called schizoaffective disorder, which caused him to feel very anxious and scared. In 2022, he got treatment for it, but it made it hard for him to keep acting.

Sadly, Chris Peluso passed away on August 16, 2023. His talent and energy on stage made him well-loved, and he is remembered for his contributions to the world of theater.

Chris Peluso’s Parents and Early Life

Born on July 1, 1985, Chris Peluso had a unique Canadian heritage but was raised in New Jersey. He held dual citizenship in both the United States and the United Kingdom.

Chris’s debut as a professional actor took place with a significant production of “1776” at the historic Ford Theatre.

This opportunity came about through a phone call from Director David Bell, who had previously involved Chris in a production of “THE TEMPEST” at the Shakespeare Festival of Dallas.

This marked Chris’s first job beyond high school, and his role as the Courier in “1776” left him with a fantastic experience and a debt of gratitude to his friend and mentor, David Bell.
